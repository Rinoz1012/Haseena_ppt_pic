Main Notes
Constitutional Provisions
Art 350B, inserted by the 7th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1956, provides for a Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities, commonly known as the Commissioner for Linguistic Minorities!
Art 350A (a related provision) directs every State and local authority to provide adequate facilities for instruction in the mother tongue at the primary stage of education to children belonging to linguistic minority groups!
The Commissioner's office exists specifically to monitor implementation of the Art 350A safeguard, among other linguistic minority protections!
Commissioner for Linguistic Minorities
Appointed by the President!
This is a single-officer body, unlike the multi-member commissions for SCs, STs, and BCs!
Role
Investigates all matters relating to the safeguards provided for linguistic minorities under the Constitution!
Reports findings to the President, who causes the report to be laid before each House of Parliament, and also forwards it to the governments of the states concerned!
Vision & Mission
Vision — to protect the linguistic, cultural, and educational rights of linguistic minority communities across India!
Mission — to ensure effective and uniform implementation of constitutional safeguards, especially Art 350A, throughout the states!
Functions & Objectives
Monitors the implementation of linguistic safeguards across states and union territories!
Receives and addresses grievances raised by linguistic minority individuals or groups!
Advises Union and State governments on remedial measures needed to strengthen linguistic minority protections!
Summary
The Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities (Commissioner for Linguistic Minorities) was established under Art 350B, inserted by the 7th Amendment 1956, to monitor safeguards for linguistic minorities — especially the Art 350A guarantee of mother-tongue primary education. Appointed by the President as a single-officer body (unlike multi-member SC/ST/BC commissions), the Commissioner investigates linguistic-minority safeguard issues and reports to the President, who tables the report in Parliament and shares it with concerned states. Vision/mission centers on protecting linguistic minority rights and ensuring effective implementation of Art 350A; functions include monitoring, grievance redressal, and advising governments on remedial action.
