Main Notes
Eastern India — Bengal, Assam, and Orissa
These eastern regions maintained largely independent political trajectories during this period, each developing distinct regional kingdoms relatively separate from the fragmenting Delhi Sultanate!
Western India — Gujarat
Gujarat emerged as an important and prosperous regional sultanate, with Mahmud Begarha standing out as a notable ruler who significantly strengthened the kingdom's regional power and influence!
Malwa, Mewar
These central and western Indian regional powers played significant roles in the broader political landscape of North India during this period of Delhi Sultanate fragmentation!
Northwest and North India — The Sharqis and the Lodi Sultans
The Sharqi dynasty ruled the Jaunpur Sultanate as an important regional power!
The Lodi Sultans represented the final dynasty of the Delhi Sultanate, ruling until the establishment of Mughal rule!
Kashmir
Kashmir maintained its own distinct and largely separate regional political trajectory throughout this period!
Summary
Between 1400-1525, as the Delhi Sultanate's central authority weakened significantly, numerous regional powers rose across North India: Bengal, Assam, and Orissa in the east; Gujarat (notably under Mahmud Begarha) in the west; Malwa and Mewar in central/western regions; the Sharqi Jaunpur Sultanate and the Lodi Sultans (the last Delhi Sultanate dynasty) in the north; and Kashmir maintaining its own separate trajectory. This political fragmentation set the stage for the subsequent Mughal conquest of North India.
