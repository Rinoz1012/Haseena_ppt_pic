Main Notes
Establishment & Composition
Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993 enables state governments to establish a State Human Rights Commission (SHRC)!
SHRC consists of a Chairperson — after the 2019 Amendment, a serving or retired Chief Justice of a High Court, or a serving or retired High Court judge — and 2 other members!
Appointed by the Governor on the recommendation of a committee comprising the Chief Minister, Speaker of the State Assembly, State Home Minister, and Leader of Opposition!
Functions
SHRC performs functions similar to NHRC but confined to subjects in the State List and Concurrent List!
It cannot inquire into matters already being examined by NHRC, or matters pending before a court of law!
Working
Term reduced from 5 years to 3 years (or 70 years of age, whichever earlier) by the 2019 Amendment; reappointment is now permitted!
Removal, despite being appointed by the Governor, can only be done by the PRESIDENT, following the same process as for NHRC members (misbehaviour requires reference to the Supreme Court)!
Human Rights Court
PHRA provides that the State Government may, with the concurrence of the Chief Justice of the High Court, specify a Court of Session in each district to function as a Human Rights Court!
A Special Public Prosecutor is appointed for the purpose of conducting cases in this court!
Aim — speedy trial of offences arising from violation of human rights!
2019 Amendment Act
Reduced the term of NHRC/SHRC Chairperson and members from 5 years to 3 years, while allowing reappointment!
Expanded eligibility for Chairperson — serving/retired CJI or SC judges (NHRC) and serving/retired Chief Justices of High Courts (SHRC), broadening the pool of eligible candidates!
Increased NHRC's full-time members from 2 to 3, mandating at least one woman member!
Added more ex-officio members to NHRC (Chairpersons of additional commissions)!
Empowered the Central Government to determine salary, allowances, and service conditions of SHRC Chairperson/members as well — reducing states' control over their own human rights commissions, a point of federal concern!
Summary
SHRC (PHRA 1993) has a Chairperson (serving/retired HC Chief Justice or judge, post-2019 Amendment) + 2 members, appointed by the Governor on a state committee's recommendation, but removable only by the President following the NHRC-style process. Functions mirror NHRC but are limited to State/Concurrent List matters, excluding cases already with NHRC or sub judice. Term reduced to 3 years/70 yrs by the 2019 Amendment, with reappointment now allowed. States may specify a Human Rights Court in each district (with HC Chief Justice's concurrence) for speedy trial of human rights offences. The 2019 Amendment Act broadened Chairperson eligibility, increased NHRC membership, and — controversially — shifted control over SHRC salary/service conditions to the Centre.
