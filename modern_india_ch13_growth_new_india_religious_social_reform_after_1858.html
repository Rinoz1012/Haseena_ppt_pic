Main Notes
The Arya Samaj
Founded by Swami Dayananda Saraswati in 1875, the Arya Samaj advocated a return to what it considered pure Vedic principles, explicitly opposing idol worship and various caste rigidities, and promoting a reformist reinterpretation of Hindu religious tradition!
The Ramakrishna Mission
Founded by Swami Vivekananda, building on the teachings of his spiritual guru Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, this movement promoted Vedanta philosophy alongside active social service, gaining significant influence both within India and internationally!
The Theosophical Society
This organization promoted the comparative study of world religions and attracted a diverse membership from both India and the West, contributing meaningfully to India's broader religious and cultural revival during this period!
Continued Social Reform Focus
Building on earlier 19th century reform efforts, this period saw continued focus on women's education, ongoing advocacy for widow remarriage, and various efforts toward caste reform!
The Aligarh Movement
Led by Sir Syed Ahmed Khan, this movement specifically promoted modern education among Indian Muslims, most notably through the founding of the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College (which later became Aligarh Muslim University)!
Overall Significance
These various religious and social reform movements combined elements of religious revivalism with genuine social reform, collectively contributing to a growing sense of cultural self-confidence among Indians that would significantly feed into the broader nationalist political movement!
Summary
Religious and social reform after 1858 saw the emergence of several significant movements: the Arya Samaj (Swami Dayananda Saraswati, 1875), advocating Vedic revivalism against idol worship and caste rigidity; the Ramakrishna Mission (Swami Vivekananda), promoting Vedanta philosophy and social service; and the Theosophical Society, fostering comparative religious study. Continued efforts addressed women's education and widow remarriage, while Sir Syed Ahmed Khan's Aligarh Movement specifically promoted modern education among Muslims, founding what became Aligarh Muslim University. Collectively, these movements combined religious revivalism with social reform, building the cultural self-confidence that would significantly feed into India's broader nationalist political movement.
