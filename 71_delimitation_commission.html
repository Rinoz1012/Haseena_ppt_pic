Main Notes
Rationale of Delimitation
Delimitation is the process of fixing or redrawing the boundaries of territorial constituencies for the Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies!
Its purpose is to reflect changes in population distribution over time and ensure roughly equal representation for each constituency, in line with the "one vote, one value" principle!
Commissions Established So Far
Four Delimitation Commissions have been constituted so far — in 1952, 1963, 1973, and 2002 — based on the Delimitation Acts of 1952, 1962, 1972, and 2002 respectively!
Constitutional Provisions
Art 82 empowers Parliament to enact a Delimitation Act after every Census!
Art 170 similarly governs delimitation of State Assembly constituencies!
Art 329 places delimitation orders beyond the scope of judicial review — they cannot be challenged in any court!
Fourth Delimitation Commission
Constituted under the Delimitation Act, 2002, based on the 2001 Census, and chaired by a retired Supreme Court judge, with the Chief Election Commissioner and concerned State Election Commissioners as ex-officio members!
Its recommendations were implemented starting 2008 and applied from the 2009 general elections onward!
Importantly, it did NOT increase the total number of Lok Sabha or Assembly seats — total seats have been frozen since the 42nd Amendment (1976), and this freeze was extended by the 84th and 87th Amendments until the first Census conducted after 2026!
Instead, it redrew constituency boundaries within the existing number of seats to reflect internal population shifts, and updated the number of seats reserved for SC/ST based on updated population data!
Implementation of Recommendations
Orders of the Delimitation Commission have the force of law and cannot be challenged in any court!
They are laid before the Lok Sabha and the concerned State Legislative Assembly, but these bodies cannot make any modifications to them!
Delimitation in J&K
Jammu & Kashmir was excluded from the purview of the 2002 Delimitation Commission because it had separate delimitation provisions under its own constitution (prior to the abrogation of Article 370)!
Following the J&K Reorganisation Act, 2019, a separate Delimitation Commission was constituted in 2020 specifically for the J&K Union Territory!
This Commission, based on the 2011 Census, increased the number of J&K Assembly seats from 83 to 90!
Order of the Commission
The Commission publishes its draft proposals and invites objections/suggestions from the public!
It holds public sittings to consider these objections, before finally notifying its orders in the Official Gazette, at which point the orders acquire the force of law!
Summary
Delimitation redraws constituency boundaries after each Census to maintain "one vote, one value," governed by Art 82 (Lok Sabha) and Art 170 (State Assemblies), with orders placed beyond judicial review by Art 329. Four Delimitation Commissions have been constituted so far (1952, 1963, 1973, 2002); the 4th Commission (2002 Act, based on 2001 Census) redrew boundaries and updated SC/ST reservations without increasing total seat numbers, since seats have been frozen since the 42nd Amendment (1976), extended by the 84th/87th Amendments until the first Census after 2026. Delimitation Commission orders have the force of law, cannot be challenged in court, and cannot be modified even by the legislatures before which they are laid. J&K was excluded from the 2002 Commission due to its separate constitutional provisions; a distinct Delimitation Commission was constituted in 2020 under the J&K Reorganisation Act 2019, increasing its Assembly seats from 83 to 90 based on the 2011 Census. The Commission's process involves publishing draft proposals, inviting objections, holding public sittings, and finally notifying orders in the Official Gazette.
