Main Notes
The Advent of the Aryans
The Indo-Aryans are generally believed to have entered the Indian subcontinent through the northwestern mountain passes, most likely through a process of gradual migration rather than a sudden violent invasion — the older "Aryan invasion" theory has been largely discredited by modern scholarship!
The Rigveda
The Rigveda is the oldest of the four Vedas, a collection of hymns dedicated to various deities, composed roughly between 1500-1000 BCE and organized into 10 books called Mandalas!
Among Rigvedic deities, Indra (the war god) receives the most hymns, followed by Agni (the fire god); other important deities include Varuna (associated with cosmic order and water), Surya (the sun), and Usha (dawn)!
Rigvedic Economy and Society
The Rigvedic economy was primarily pastoral, with cattle serving as the chief measure of wealth, supplemented by some agriculture, particularly the cultivation of barley!
Many recorded battles of this period revolved around cattle raids between rival tribes!
Society was organized along tribal, kinship-based lines and was patriarchal, though women enjoyed a relatively better status than in later periods — they could attend tribal assemblies, and some women are credited with composing hymns; importantly, there was no rigid varna (caste) system during this period!
Political Organization
The tribal chief, called the Rajan, was not an absolute monarch but was assisted and checked by tribal assemblies — the Sabha and Samiti — which incorporated early democratic elements into governance!
The chief was supported by officials such as the Purohita (priest) and the Senani (military leader) in administering the tribe!
The Battle of Ten Kings (Dasarajna)
The Rigveda describes the famous "Dasarajna" (Battle of Ten Kings), fought on the banks of the river Parushni (identified with the modern Ravi), in which King Sudas of the Bharata tribe defeated a confederation of ten rival tribes — a key historical episode from this period!
Summary
The Indo-Aryans likely entered India through northwestern passes via gradual migration (not a sudden invasion, as the older theory suggested). The Rigveda, the oldest of the four Vedas (c. 1500-1000 BCE, 10 Mandalas), centers on hymns to deities like Indra (most-hymned, war god), Agni, Varuna, Surya, and Usha. Rigvedic society was pastoral (cattle-based wealth), tribal, and patriarchal but without a rigid varna system, with relatively better status for women. Political organization featured a tribal chief (Rajan) checked by the Sabha and Samiti assemblies, assisted by a Purohita and Senani. The Battle of Ten Kings (Dasarajna), where King Sudas defeated a ten-tribe confederation on the river Parushni, stands as a key historical event of this era.
