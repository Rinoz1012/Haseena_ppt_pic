Main Notes
Establishment
NCBC was originally established as a statutory (non-constitutional) body under the National Commission for Backward Classes Act, 1993!
The 102nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 2018 inserted Art 338B, elevating NCBC to constitutional status, parallel to NCSC and NCST!
This made NCBC a constitutional body responsible for safeguarding the interests of socially and educationally backward classes (SEBCs/OBCs)!
Functions
Monitors implementation of constitutional and legal safeguards provided for backward classes!
Inquires into specific complaints relating to deprivation of rights and safeguards of backward classes!
Participates in and advises on the planning process for the socio-economic development of backward classes!
Report
Submits an annual report (and special reports as needed) to the President!
President causes the report to be laid before Parliament, accompanied by a memorandum on the action taken and reasons for non-acceptance of recommendations!
Power
While investigating any complaint, NCBC has all the powers of a civil court — summoning witnesses, requiring document production, and receiving evidence on affidavit!
A distinctive additional function of NCBC — examining requests for inclusion of new castes/communities in, or exclusion of existing ones from, the Central List of OBCs, and tendering advice to the Central Government accordingly!
Summary
NCBC began as a statutory body under the National Commission for Backward Classes Act, 1993, and was elevated to constitutional status by the 102nd Amendment 2018 (Art 338B). Composition = Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 members, President-appointed. Functions = monitoring backward-class safeguards, inquiring into complaints, advising on socio-economic development, plus a distinctive role examining inclusion/exclusion requests for the OBC Central List. Reports go to the President, then Parliament with an action-taken memorandum; powers = civil court powers while investigating.
