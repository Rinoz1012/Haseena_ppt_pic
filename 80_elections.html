Main Notes
Electoral System
India uses the First-Past-The-Post (FPTP) system for direct elections to the Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies — the country is divided into territorial constituencies, and the candidate securing the most votes in a constituency wins, without needing an absolute majority (over 50%)!
For indirect elections — such as to the Rajya Sabha and State Legislative Councils — India uses the Single Transferable Vote (STV) system, a form of proportional representation, where MLAs cast votes that can transfer to lower-preference candidates once higher preferences are elected/eliminated!
Election Machinery
The Election Commission of India (Art 324) has overall superintendence, direction, and control over the conduct of elections!
A Returning Officer is designated for each constituency, responsible for conducting the election there and declaring the result!
A Presiding Officer is appointed for each polling station to conduct voting there!
An Electoral Registration Officer is responsible for preparing and revising the electoral rolls for each constituency!
Election Process
The process begins with the ECI issuing a notification announcing the election schedule, which immediately brings the Model Code of Conduct into force!
This is followed by filing of nominations by candidates, scrutiny of nomination papers by the Returning Officer, and a period for withdrawal of candidature!
Voting takes place using Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) along with Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail (VVPAT) machines, which allow voters to visually confirm their vote!
After polling concludes, votes are counted, and results are formally declared by the Returning Officer!
Candidates are required to pay a security deposit at the time of filing nomination, and are allotted an election symbol (either a reserved party symbol or a free symbol for independents)!
Summary
India uses FPTP for direct elections to the Lok Sabha and State Assemblies (highest vote-getter wins, no majority required), while indirect elections to the Rajya Sabha and Legislative Councils use the proportional STV system. Election machinery involves the ECI (overall superintendence), Returning Officers (constituency-level), Presiding Officers (polling stations), and Electoral Registration Officers (electoral rolls). The election process runs through notification (triggering the Model Code of Conduct), nomination/scrutiny/withdrawal, polling via EVM+VVPAT, and finally counting and declaration of results.
