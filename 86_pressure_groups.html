Main Notes
Meaning and Techniques
A pressure group is an organized group of individuals or organizations that seeks to influence government policy and decision-making, without directly seeking to capture political power through elections — this distinguishes them fundamentally from political parties!
Techniques used by pressure groups include lobbying legislators and government officials, organizing public campaigns, protests, and demonstrations, and engaging in media advocacy to shape public opinion!
They also resort to litigation — such as filing Public Interest Litigations (PILs) — to advance their cause through the judiciary!
Other techniques include funding candidates or parties sympathetic to their cause (where legally permissible), submitting expert testimony to government committees, and, in more extreme cases, organizing strikes or bandhs to pressure the government!
Pressure Groups in India
India has a wide variety of pressure groups, including business and trade associations (such as FICCI and CII) that advocate for industry/commercial interests!
Trade unions represent labour interests and often use strikes and collective bargaining as pressure tactics!
Farmer organizations have historically mobilized significant political pressure around agricultural policy, pricing, and land issues!
Caste and community associations, religious organizations, and student/professional bodies also function as pressure groups, advocating for their respective communities' or sectors' interests in the policymaking process!
Summary
Pressure groups are organized entities that seek to influence government policy without directly contesting for political power, distinguishing them from political parties. They use techniques like lobbying, public campaigns, media advocacy, litigation (including PILs), and, in extreme cases, strikes. In India, pressure groups span business/trade associations (FICCI, CII), trade unions, farmer organizations, caste/community associations, religious organizations, and student/professional bodies — each advocating for their sector's or community's interests in shaping public policy.
