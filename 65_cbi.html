Main Notes
Establishment
CBI was not created by any specific Act of Parliament — it traces its origin to the Special Police Establishment set up in 1941 to investigate corruption in wartime procurement!
The CBI itself was established through a Home Ministry Resolution in 1963, based on the recommendations of the Santhanam Committee!
It derives its legal/investigative powers from the Delhi Special Police Establishment (DSPE) Act, 1946 — the only statute currently governing its functioning!
Motto, Mission & Vision
Motto — "Industry, Impartiality, Integrity"!
Vision — to be a highly respected organization with a global reputation for excellence, on par with the best investigative agencies in the world!
Mission — to uphold the Constitution and law, maintain the integrity of the financial, economic, and social fabric of the country, and provide leadership/direction in fighting corruption and economic/violent crime through eminent, competent, and professional investigation!
Composition
Headed by a Director, holding the rank of Director General of Police!
Following amendments made via the Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 2013 to the DSPE Act, the Director is appointed by the Central Government on the recommendation of a committee comprising the Prime Minister, Chief Justice of India (or a SC judge nominated by him), and the Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha!
Following Supreme Court directions in the Vineet Narain case and the CVC Act, the Director has a minimum fixed tenure of 2 years, to insulate the post from arbitrary transfers!
Organized into divisions such as the Anti-Corruption Division, Economic Offences Division, and Special Crimes Division!
Functions
Investigates cases of corruption and bribery involving central government employees and public sector undertakings!
Investigates serious, sensational crimes referred to it by state governments or the courts, especially those with inter-state or international ramifications!
Handles significant economic offences, and coordinates the activities of various anti-corruption agencies!
Provisions of Prior Permission
Section 6 of the DSPE Act requires CBI to obtain the consent of the concerned state government before investigating a case within that state's territory!
This consent can be "general" (a standing consent covering all cases) or "case-specific" (given individually for each case)!
In recent years, several state governments have withdrawn their general consent (often amid political disputes with the Centre), requiring CBI to seek permission on a case-by-case basis before investigating within that state!
CBI vs State Police
State police handles general law and order maintenance and investigation of crimes under state jurisdiction (IPC and local/state laws)!
CBI is a central agency primarily focused on corruption by central government employees, and interstate/economic/high-profile crimes referred by courts or state governments — but its jurisdiction within a state is subject to that state's consent (except in Union Territories, where no such consent is required)!
CBI is not accountable to any state legislature, and its functioning has faced repeated criticism over alleged political interference — the Supreme Court famously described it as a "caged parrot" in the 2013 coal allocation scam case!
Summary
CBI has no dedicated statute — it operates under the Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, having been formally set up via a 1963 Home Ministry Resolution, tracing its roots to the 1941 Special Police Establishment. Motto: "Industry, Impartiality, Integrity"; Vision/Mission emphasize professional, globally respected anti-corruption investigation. Headed by a Director (DGP rank) appointed via a PM-CJI-LOP committee (post-Lokpal Act 2013 amendment) with a minimum 2-year tenure (Vineet Narain case). Functions cover corruption by central employees, referred serious/economic crimes, and inter-state/international cases. Its jurisdiction within a state requires state consent under DSPE Act Section 6 (general or case-specific) — several states have withdrawn general consent, a major Centre-State friction point. Unlike state police, CBI is not accountable to any state legislature, and has faced the "caged parrot" criticism regarding political interference.
