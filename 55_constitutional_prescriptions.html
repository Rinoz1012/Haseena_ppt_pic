55. Constitutional Prescriptions — Comparative Cornell Notes 06 Jul 2026
Keywords
Appointing Authority President: EC, UPSC, FC, GST Council(via), NCSC, NCST, NCBC, Linguistic Comm., CAG, AG! SPSC/Advocate General = GOVERNOR!
Age Qualification AG=SC judge qualified! Advocate General=HC judge qualified! Others = no specific "age" bar, but retire at 65 (EC, UPSC/SPSC, CAG)!
Oath Most constitutional functionaries take oath before assuming office (specifics vary)! AG/Advocate General = no separate constitutional oath format prescribed!
Term Pattern
6yrs/65yrs = EC, UPSC, SPSC, CAG (FIXED-term bodies) No fixed term = AG, Advocate General (pleasure of President/Governor) 5 yrs (or earlier) = Finance Commission
Removal Pattern Like SC judge (impeachment) = CEC, CAG! On CEC's recommendation = other ECs! By President after SC inquiry (misbehaviour) = UPSC/SPSC! At pleasure = AG/Advocate General (NOT "removal", simply withdrawal of pleasure)!
Salary Charged On Consolidated Fund of INDIA = EC, UPSC, CAG! Consolidated Fund of STATE = SPSC! AG/Advocate General = determined by President/Governor, not fixed in Constitution!
Resignation All can resign by writing to appointing authority (President/Governor)!
Reports to Parliament
President→Parliament: EC(indirectly via elections), UPSC, FC, NCSC, NCST, NCBC, Linguistic Comm., CAG Governor→State Legislature: SPSC, CAG(state portion)
Dissolution — N/A here Most of these bodies are PERMANENT, not subject to "dissolution" like Houses of Parliament! Finance Commission is reconstituted every 5 yrs (not "dissolved")!
PYQ Alert
AG/Advocate General = pleasure-tenure, no fixed term, no SC-judge-style removal process! Most others = fixed term + structured removal!
Main Notes — Comparison Across Bodies
Composition
Multi-member Election Commission (CEC+ECs), UPSC/SPSC (Chairman+members), Finance Commission (Chairman+4), GST Council (FM+state ministers), NCSC/NCST/NCBC (Chairperson+VC+3 members)!
Single-officer CAG, Attorney General, Advocate General, Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities!
Appointments
By PRESIDENT: Election Commission, UPSC, Finance Commission, NCSC, NCST, NCBC, Linguistic Minorities Commissioner, CAG, Attorney General!
By GOVERNOR: SPSC members, Advocate General of State!
GST Council — not "appointed" as individuals; constituted by President, but composed of sitting Finance Ministers (ex-officio)!
Age
Retirement age of 65 applies to: CEC/ECs, UPSC/SPSC members, CAG!
Attorney General must qualify to be a SC judge; Advocate General must qualify to be a HC judge — these are qualification thresholds, not retirement ages!
NCSC/NCST/NCBC members — no specific constitutional age limit; tenure/conditions set by President through rules!
Oaths
Most high constitutional functionaries subscribe to an oath/affirmation of office (format & administering authority vary by body/statute)!
Attorney General and Advocate General do not have a separately prescribed constitutional oath format like the President, Governor, or judges!
Term
Fixed 6 years/65 years (whichever earlier) — Election Commission, UPSC, SPSC, CAG!
Finance Commission — reconstituted every 5 years, or earlier if the President considers necessary!
Attorney General & Advocate General — NO fixed term; hold office at the pleasure of the President/Governor respectively!
Salaries
Charged on the Consolidated Fund of India (non-votable) — Election Commission, UPSC, CAG!
Charged on the Consolidated Fund of the State — SPSC!
Attorney General/Advocate General remuneration — determined by the President/Governor respectively, not fixed by the Constitution itself!
Resignations
All these functionaries may resign by addressing a resignation letter to their respective appointing authority (President or Governor)!
Removals
Like a Supreme Court judge (impeachment process) — CEC, CAG!
Other Election Commissioners — removed by President only on CEC's recommendation!
UPSC/SPSC members — removed by President; misbehaviour ground needs a Supreme Court inquiry, other grounds (insolvency, outside paid job, infirmity) allow direct removal!
Attorney General/Advocate General — hold office at pleasure; ending their tenure is a withdrawal of pleasure, not a formal "removal" process!
Dissolutions
These are largely PERMANENT constitutional bodies/offices — "dissolution" in the sense applicable to legislative Houses does not apply!
Finance Commission is periodically reconstituted (every 5 years) rather than dissolved!
Submission of Reports
President-routed reports, laid before Parliament: UPSC, Finance Commission, NCSC, NCST, NCBC, Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities, CAG (Union accounts)!
Governor-routed reports, laid before State Legislature: SPSC, CAG (State accounts portion)!
Attorney General/Advocate General do not submit periodic constitutional reports — their role is advisory/representational, not report-based!
Summary
Across constitutional bodies: appointment authority is mostly the President (EC, UPSC, FC, NCSC/NCST/NCBC, Linguistic Commissioner, CAG, AG), except SPSC and Advocate General (Governor). Fixed 6yr/65yr terms apply to EC, UPSC, SPSC, CAG; Finance Commission is reconstituted every 5 years; AG and Advocate General instead serve at the pleasure of their appointing authority with no fixed term. Removal is structured (SC-judge-style impeachment for CEC/CAG; SC-inquiry-based for UPSC/SPSC misbehaviour) for most bodies, but is a simple "pleasure withdrawal" for AG/Advocate General. Salaries are charged on the Consolidated Fund of India (Union bodies) or of the State (SPSC), while AG/Advocate General remuneration is discretionary. Reports flow President→Parliament or Governor→State Legislature for most bodies, but AG/Advocate General have no report-submission role since they function as legal advisors, not oversight bodies. Most of these bodies are permanent and not subject to "dissolution."
