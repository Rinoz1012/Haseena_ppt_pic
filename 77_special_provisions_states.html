Main Notes
Maharashtra & Gujarat
Art 371 gives the Governor a special responsibility for establishing separate development boards for Vidarbha, Marathwada, and the rest of Maharashtra, and similarly for Saurashtra, Kutch, and the rest of Gujarat!
It also requires equitable allocation of funds for developmental expenditure across these regions, and equitable arrangements for adequate technical education, vocational training, and employment opportunities in state services!
Nagaland & Mizoram
Art 371A (Nagaland) and Art 371G (Mizoram) follow an identical structure — no Act of Parliament affecting the religious or social practices of the Nagas/Mizos, their customary law and procedure, administration of civil and criminal justice according to Naga/Mizo customary law, or ownership and transfer of land and its resources, shall apply to that state UNLESS the state Legislative Assembly decides by a resolution to adopt it!
For Nagaland specifically, the Governor also has special responsibility for law and order in the Naga Hills-Tuensang Area as long as internal disturbance persists, along with the establishment of a Regional Council for the Tuensang district!
Assam & Manipur
Art 371B allows the President to provide for the constitution and functions of a committee of the Assam Legislative Assembly, consisting of members elected from the tribal areas of Assam, to assist in modification of laws applicable to those areas!
Art 371C makes a similar provision for Manipur — a committee of the state Assembly consisting of members elected from the Hill Areas of Manipur, with the Governor bearing special responsibility to ensure its proper functioning and to report to the President!
Andhra Pradesh / Telangana
Art 371D empowers the President to provide for equitable opportunities and facilities for people of different parts of the state in matters of public employment and education, and for the establishment of an Administrative Tribunal to deal with state civil service disputes!
Art 371E, added by the 32nd Amendment Act, 1973, provides for the establishment of a Central University in Andhra Pradesh!
Sikkim
Art 371F was added by the 36th Amendment Act, 1975, following Sikkim's merger with India!
It provides for one Lok Sabha seat reserved for Sikkim, protects the representation of different sections of Sikkim's population in the state Legislative Assembly, gives the Governor special responsibility for peace and for the socio-economic advancement of different population sections, and allows existing Sikkim laws to continue in force until amended or repealed!
Arunachal Pradesh & Goa
Art 371H gives the Governor of Arunachal Pradesh special responsibility with respect to law and order in the state, requiring the Governor to act in his individual judgment after consulting the Council of Ministers!
Art 371I fixes the minimum number of members in the Goa Legislative Assembly at 30!
Karnataka
Art 371J, added by the 98th Amendment Act, 2012, provides special provisions for the Hyderabad-Karnataka region — establishment of a separate development board, equitable allocation of funds for developmental expenditure, and reservation in education, vocational training, and state government employment for people from that region!
Summary
Part XXI (Art 371 to 371-J) provides state-specific special provisions. Maharashtra/Gujarat (Art 371) get separate regional development boards and equitable resource allocation. Nagaland (371A) and Mizoram (371G) share identical protections shielding religious/social practices, customary law, and land ownership from Parliament's laws unless their own Assembly consents. Assam (371B) and Manipur (371C) get tribal/hill-area legislative committees. Andhra Pradesh/Telangana (371D, 371E) address regional employment/education equity and a Central University. Sikkim (371F, added 1975 post-merger) reserves a Lok Sabha seat and protects population-section representation. Arunachal Pradesh (371H) gives the Governor special law-and-order responsibility exercised in individual judgment. Goa (371I) fixes a minimum 30-member Assembly. Karnataka (371J, added 2012) provides development and employment safeguards for the Hyderabad-Karnataka region.
