Main Notes
Composition
SIC consists of a State Chief Information Commissioner and up to 10 State Information Commissioners!
Appointed by the Governor on the recommendation of a committee comprising the Chief Minister (Chairperson), the Leader of Opposition in the State Assembly, and a State Cabinet Minister nominated by the Chief Minister!
Tenure & Service Conditions
Under the original RTI Act 2005, SIC members had a fixed 5-year term (or age 65), with salary parity roughly tied to Election Commissioner/Chief Secretary levels!
The RTI (Amendment) Act, 2019 removed this fixed term for SIC as well — term of office, salary, allowances, and other service conditions are now prescribed by the CENTRAL GOVERNMENT via rules, applicable uniformly to both CIC and SIC!
This is a significant federal concern — even though SICs are state-level bodies dealing with state RTI matters, their service conditions are now controlled by the Union government rather than the concerned state government!
Powers & Functions
SIC exercises the same range of powers as CIC, but confined to public authorities under state government jurisdiction!
Receives and inquires into complaints/second appeals relating to denial of information, delays, or unreasonable fees by state PIOs!
Has civil-court-like powers while inquiring — summoning, requiring documents, receiving evidence on affidavit!
Can impose penalties on non-compliant state PIOs and recommend disciplinary action; publishes reports on RTI implementation at the state level!
RTI Amendment Act 2019
Removed the fixed 5-year term for both CIC and SIC members — term now to be prescribed by Central Government rules!
Removed the earlier parity of SIC/CIC salary with Election Commissioners, giving the Central Government the power to fix salary/allowances/service conditions instead!
Established uniform rules governing both central and state Information Commissions, which critics argue centralizes control over what were meant to be independent bodies (especially concerning for state-level SICs), potentially compromising their autonomy!
Summary
SIC (RTI Act, 2005) is composed of a State Chief Information Commissioner + up to 10 State Information Commissioners, appointed by the Governor on a state-level committee's recommendation. Originally a fixed 5-year term with Election Commissioner/Chief Secretary-level salary parity, but the RTI (Amendment) Act, 2019 removed this fixed term and shifted control over term, salary, and service conditions to the CENTRAL GOVERNMENT for both CIC and SIC — a major point of controversy given SIC's state-level mandate. Powers/functions mirror CIC at the state level: receiving complaints/appeals, civil-court investigative powers, imposing penalties on state PIOs, and publishing reports. The 2019 Amendment is widely seen as centralizing control and potentially undermining the independence of Information Commissions, especially at the state level.
