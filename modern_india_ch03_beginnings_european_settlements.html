Main Notes
The Portuguese
The Portuguese were the first Europeans to establish a direct trading presence in India, following Vasco da Gama's landmark voyage in 1498; Goa became their key base and remained a Portuguese possession for centuries!
The Dutch
The Dutch established their own trading posts in India, competing with the Portuguese and later the English for control of the lucrative spice and textile trade!
The English (East India Company)
The English East India Company was founded in 1600 and gradually established a network of trading posts, known as factories, at key locations including Surat, Madras, Bombay, and Calcutta!
The French
The French established their own East India Company somewhat later, with Pondicherry serving as their key base of operations in India!
Anglo-French Rivalry
Intense competition for trade and regional influence between the English and French eventually escalated into direct military conflict, particularly through the Carnatic Wars fought in South India!
Evolution of Trading Posts
Over time, these initially purely commercial trading posts (factories) evolved into fortified settlements, and the various European companies increasingly developed political and territorial ambitions beyond simple trade!
Summary
European trading presence in India began with the Portuguese (Vasco da Gama, 1498, based at Goa), followed by the Dutch, and then the English East India Company (founded 1600, establishing factories at Surat, Madras, Bombay, and Calcutta) and the French East India Company (based at Pondicherry). Intense Anglo-French rivalry for trade and influence eventually led to direct military conflict through the Carnatic Wars in South India. Over this period, these initially commercial trading posts gradually evolved into fortified settlements with growing political and territorial ambitions, setting the stage for eventual colonial conquest.
