Main Notes
Political and Administrative Developments in India; Jahangir
Jahangir, succeeding his father Akbar, generally continued the administrative framework established during Akbar's reign, introducing some modifications while maintaining overall continuity!
Nur Jahan
Jahangir's influential and capable wife, Nur Jahan, played a significant and at times decisive role in court politics and administrative decision-making during his reign!
Shah Jahan's Rebellion; Mahabat Khan
Shah Jahan, who would later become emperor, rebelled against his father Jahangir at one point during this period!
The noble Mahabat Khan was also involved in significant political intrigue and conflict during Jahangir's reign!
Foreign Policy of the Mughals — Shah Jahan's Balkh Campaign
Shah Jahan pursued an ambitious military campaign aimed at Balkh in Central Asia, though this ultimately proved unsuccessful and represented a significant drain on Mughal resources!
Mughal-Persian Relations — the Last Phase
Relations between the Mughal Empire and the Persian Safavid Empire remained complex throughout this period, evolving through various phases of cooperation and tension!
Growth of Administration — Mansabdari System and the Mughal Army
This period saw continued development and refinement of the Mansabdari system and the broader organization of the Mughal army, building on Akbar's foundational reforms!
Summary
The first half of the 17th century saw continuity in Mughal administration under Jahangir, with his influential wife Nur Jahan playing a significant political role, alongside political tensions including Shah Jahan's rebellion and Mahabat Khan's intrigues. Shah Jahan's later ambitious but unsuccessful Balkh campaign in Central Asia represented a significant, largely unproductive foreign policy venture, while Mughal-Persian relations remained complex throughout. This period also saw continued refinement of the Mansabdari system and Mughal military organization, building on Akbar's earlier administrative foundations.
