Main Notes
Representation of the People Act, 1950
This Act primarily deals with the allocation of seats in the Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies, delimitation of constituencies, qualifications of voters, and the preparation of electoral rolls!
It essentially governs matters relating to who can VOTE, and how constituencies are structured!
Representation of the People Act, 1951
This Act deals with the actual conduct of elections, qualifications and disqualifications for contesting as a candidate, corrupt practices and electoral offences, resolution of election disputes through Election Petitions, and the registration of political parties!
Essentially, it governs who can STAND for election and how the election process itself is conducted!
Delimitation Act, 2002
Formed the legal basis for the Fourth Delimitation Commission (already covered in the Delimitation Commission chapter), which redrew constituency boundaries based on the 2001 Census without increasing the total number of seats!
Other Acts, Rules and Orders Relating to Elections
The Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952 governs the specific procedures for electing the President and Vice-President!
The Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968 — an ECI order rather than a parliamentary Act — governs the reservation and allotment of symbols to recognized and other parties!
The Conduct of Election Rules, 1961 provides detailed procedural rules covering nomination forms, counting procedures, and use of EVMs/VVPATs!
Various other ECI orders are issued from time to time to address symbol allotment disputes, enforcement of the Model Code of Conduct, and notification of bye-elections!
Summary
The Representation of the People Act, 1950 governs seat allocation, constituency delimitation, voter qualifications, and electoral rolls — essentially the "who can vote" framework. The Representation of the People Act, 1951 governs the actual conduct of elections, candidate qualifications/disqualifications, corrupt practices, election disputes, and party registration — the "who can stand and how elections run" framework. The Delimitation Act, 2002 provided the basis for the 4th Delimitation Commission. Supporting this framework are the Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, the Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968, the Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, and various other ECI orders governing procedural and operational aspects of elections.
