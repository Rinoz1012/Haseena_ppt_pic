Main Notes
Establishment & Rationale
NITI Aayog (National Institution for Transforming India) replaced the Planning Commission (established 1950) through a Cabinet Resolution dated 1 January 2015!
Like the Planning Commission, it is NOT a constitutional or statutory body — purely an executive/advisory institution!
Rationale: move away from centralized, top-down Five-Year Plans toward a flexible, bottom-up strategy that gives states greater ownership of national development planning, reflecting India's evolving economic and federal landscape!
Composition
Chairperson = Prime Minister!
Governing Council = Chief Ministers of all states and UTs with legislatures, plus Lieutenant Governors of other UTs!
Regional Councils may be formed to address region-specific issues, convened by the PM or his nominee!
Full-time Vice-Chairperson appointed by the PM; part-time members (max 2) drawn from leading universities/research institutions; full-time members; ex-officio members (max 4 Union Council Ministers nominated by the PM); CEO with a fixed tenure, of Secretary rank; special invitees as needed!
Objective & Functions
Foster cooperative federalism through structured support to states on a continuous basis!
Develop mechanisms for formulating credible plans at the village level and aggregating these progressively at higher levels!
Ensure national security interests are incorporated into economic strategy and policy!
Pay special attention to sections of society that may be at risk of not benefiting from economic progress!
Provide a platform for resolving inter-sectoral and inter-departmental issues, and act as a resource center and knowledge/innovation hub!
Monitor and evaluate implementation of programmes, and focus on technology upgradation and capacity building!
Guiding Principles
Antyodaya — uplift of the poorest and most marginalized as the first priority!
Inclusion of all sections of society, and deepening/strengthening federalism through structured cooperation!
Integration of national security concerns into economic policy, and commitment to sustainable development leveraging India's demographic dividend!
Cooperative & Competitive Federalism
Cooperative federalism — states participate directly in national policymaking through the Governing Council and thematic sub-groups of Chief Ministers!
Competitive federalism — NITI Aayog encourages healthy competition among states through comparative indices like the SDG India Index, Health Index, and Ease of Doing Business rankings, incentivizing better governance!
Autonomous & Attached Bodies
National Institute of Labour Economics Research and Development (NILERD) — attached body focused on labour economics research!
Development Monitoring and Evaluation Office (DMEO) — attached office responsible for monitoring/evaluating government schemes and programmes!
Summary
NITI Aayog (Cabinet Resolution, 1 Jan 2015) replaced the Planning Commission; extra-constitutional/extra-statutory like its predecessor. Rationale = shift from top-down planning to bottom-up, state-driven strategy. Composition: PM as Chairperson, Governing Council of CMs/LGs, Vice-Chairperson, part-time/full-time/ex-officio members, and a Secretary-rank CEO. Objectives/functions center on cooperative federalism, village-level planning, national security integration, and support for marginalized groups, guided by principles like Antyodaya and sustainable development. It promotes cooperative federalism via the Governing Council and competitive federalism via performance indices (SDG Index, Health Index). Attached/autonomous bodies include NILERD and DMEO. Unlike the Planning Commission, NITI Aayog has no power to allocate funds — it is a policy think tank.
