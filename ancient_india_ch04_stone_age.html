Main Notes
The Three Phases of the Stone Age
The Stone Age, the long period when stone was the primary tool-making material, is divided into three phases — Paleolithic (Old Stone Age), Mesolithic (Middle Stone Age), and Neolithic (New Stone Age) — classified according to tool type and the corresponding way of life!
Paleolithic Age
People during this period were nomadic hunter-gatherers, using quartzite stone to make hand axes, choppers, and cleavers!
The period is sub-divided into Lower Paleolithic (crude tools), Middle Paleolithic (smaller flake tools), and Upper Paleolithic (more refined blade and burin tools)!
Important sites include Bhimbetka (Madhya Pradesh — famous for rock shelters and paintings, a UNESCO World Heritage Site), Hunsgi (Karnataka), the Kurnool caves (Andhra Pradesh), and Didwana (Rajasthan)!
Mesolithic Age
This transitional period occurred as the climate warmed following the Ice Age, characterized by the use of microliths (small, sharp stone tools) and the beginning of animal domestication, with the dog believed to be among the earliest domesticated animals!
Key sites include Bagor (Rajasthan), Langhnaj (Gujarat), Adamgarh (Madhya Pradesh), and Sarai Nahar Rai (Uttar Pradesh), where burial evidence has also been found!
Neolithic Age
The Neolithic Age marks the beginning of agriculture — the "Neolithic Revolution" — the most transformative shift in early human history, moving communities from nomadic life to settled villages!
Tools became polished and refined, and pottery was introduced, along with domestication of both plants and animals!
Important sites include Mehrgarh (Baluchistan, Pakistan — earliest evidence of farming in the subcontinent), Burzahom (Kashmir), Chirand (Bihar), and Koldihwa (Uttar Pradesh — early rice cultivation evidence)!
Summary
The Stone Age divides into Paleolithic (nomadic hunter-gatherers using quartzite hand axes/choppers; key sites: Bhimbetka, Hunsgi, Kurnool caves, Didwana), Mesolithic (transitional, microliths, first animal domestication; key sites: Bagor, Langhnaj, Adamgarh, Sarai Nahar Rai), and Neolithic (the "Neolithic Revolution" — agriculture, settled villages, polished tools, pottery; key sites: Mehrgarh, Burzahom, Chirand, Koldihwa) phases, marking humanity's gradual transition from nomadic survival to settled agricultural life.
