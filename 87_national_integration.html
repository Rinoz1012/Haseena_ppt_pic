Main Notes
Meaning
National integration refers to the process of unifying people across India's diverse religious, caste, regional, and linguistic identities into a shared sense of national identity and belonging, transcending these sub-national loyalties!
Obstacles
Communalism (religious division and conflict) remains one of the most significant obstacles to national integration in India!
Casteism, linguistic chauvinism ("linguism"), and regionalism also create fault lines that hinder a unified national identity!
Political opportunism that exploits these social divisions for electoral gain further exacerbates these obstacles!
Economic disparities across regions, and extremism/terrorism that exploits identity-based fault lines, also pose serious challenges to national integration!
National Integration Council
The National Integration Council (NIC) is an apex advisory body established in 1961, chaired by the Prime Minister!
Its members include Chief Ministers of states, leaders of major political parties, and eminent public figures!
It serves as a platform to deliberate on and address issues of communalism, casteism, and regionalism that threaten national integration!
National Foundation for Communal Harmony
The National Foundation for Communal Harmony (NFCH) was established in 1992 as an autonomous body under the Ministry of Home Affairs!
It provides financial assistance for the rehabilitation of children (and in some cases their families) affected by communal, caste, ethnic, or terrorist violence!
It also promotes communal harmony and national integration through various awareness and educational programmes!
Summary
National integration is the process of unifying India's diverse religious, caste, regional, and linguistic groups into a shared national identity. Key obstacles include communalism, casteism, linguistic chauvinism, regionalism, political opportunism exploiting these divides, economic disparities, and extremism. The National Integration Council (1961), chaired by the PM with CMs and political leaders as members, serves as an advisory forum to address these issues. The National Foundation for Communal Harmony (1992), an autonomous body under the Ministry of Home Affairs, focuses specifically on rehabilitating victims (especially children) of communal/caste/ethnic/terrorist violence and promoting communal harmony through outreach programmes.
