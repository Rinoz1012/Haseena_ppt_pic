Main Notes
Establishment & Composition
National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) was established under the Commissions for Protection of Child Rights Act, 2005 — a statutory body!
Composed of a Chairperson — an eminent person who has done outstanding work for the welfare of children — and 6 members (at least 2 women), with expertise in areas such as education, child health/care/welfare, juvenile justice, children with disabilities, elimination of child labour, child psychology, and laws relating to children!
Functions
Examines and reviews safeguards provided for children under any law, and recommends measures for their effective implementation!
Inquires into violations of child rights and recommends initiation of proceedings in such cases!
Examines factors — including terrorism, riots, and other events — that inhibit the enjoyment of child rights, and recommends remedial measures!
Studies treaties and international instruments on child rights, undertakes research, and spreads awareness through publication of research/studies!
Inspects juvenile custodial homes and other institutions housing children, and recommends remedial measures where deficiencies are found!
Powers
While investigating complaints of deprivation/violation of child rights, NCPCR has all the powers of a civil court, similar to NCW and NHRC!
Functions Under Other Acts
Under the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009, NCPCR monitors implementation of the right to education across states!
Under the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012, NCPCR monitors the implementation of provisions meant to protect children from sexual offences!
State Commission for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCR)
States may establish their own State Commission for Protection of Child Rights, with composition and functions mirroring those of NCPCR but limited to the state level!
Children's Courts
The 2005 Act enables state governments to specify, for each district, a Children's Court for speedy trial of offences against children or violations of child rights!
Where a Children's Court is not designated, the State Government may specify an existing Court of Session in the district; a Special Public Prosecutor is also appointed for such trials!
Summary
NCPCR (Commissions for Protection of Child Rights Act, 2005) is a statutory body with a Chairperson + 6 members (≥2 women) drawn from diverse child-welfare fields. Functions include reviewing legal safeguards, inquiring into rights violations, studying international instruments, spreading awareness, and inspecting custodial homes, backed by civil-court investigative powers. It also carries specific monitoring duties under the RTE Act 2009 and POCSO Act 2012. States can establish parallel State Commissions (SCPCR), and the 2005 Act enables designation of Children's Courts (existing Sessions Courts) with Special Public Prosecutors for speedy trial of child-rights offences.
