Main Notes
Establishment
GST Council established under Art 279A, inserted by the 101st Constitutional Amendment Act, 2016!
Constituted by the President within 60 days of the Article coming into force!
Composition
Chairman = Union Finance Minister!
Members = Union Minister of State (in charge of Revenue/Finance) + one Minister nominated by each State government, in charge of Finance/Taxation!
Working — Voting & Quorum
Every decision requires a majority of not less than 3/4th of the weighted votes of members present & voting!
Centre's vote has a weightage of 1/3rd of total votes cast; all States together have a weightage of 2/3rd!
Quorum for a valid meeting = 50% of the total number of members!
Vision & Mission
Vision — establish India as a unified national market through a single, harmonized GST framework ("One Nation, One Tax")!
Mission — foster cooperative federalism by ensuring both Centre and States have a decisive say in indirect tax policy!
Functions
Recommends which taxes, cesses, and surcharges of Centre/States should be subsumed under GST!
Recommends goods/services exempted, threshold limits for GST registration, and GST rates/rate bands!
Recommends model GST laws, principles of levy, and place-of-supply rules!
Other Functions
Recommends special provisions for J&K, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and the North-Eastern states!
Under Art 279A(11), the Council is mandated to establish a mechanism to adjudicate disputes arising between the Centre and States, or among States, relating to GST recommendations!
Summary
GST Council (Art 279A, inserted by 101st Amendment 2016) is constituted by the President; Chairman = Union FM, members = Union MoS(Finance) + each state's Finance/Taxation Minister. Decisions need a 3/4th weighted majority (Centre 1/3, States 2/3 combined), quorum 50%. Vision/Mission = "One Nation One Tax" via cooperative federalism. Functions cover subsuming taxes, exemptions, thresholds, GST rates, model laws, and place-of-supply rules; other functions include special provisions for hill/NE states and a mandatory dispute-resolution mechanism (Art 279A(11)).
