Main Notes
Evolution
Originally, Art 338 (as in the original Constitution) provided for a Special Officer to investigate matters relating to safeguards for SC/STs!
65th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1990 replaced this with a multi-member National Commission for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes!
89th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2003 bifurcated this single commission into two separate bodies: National Commission for Scheduled Castes (NCSC, Art 338) and National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST, Art 338A)!
Composition
NCSC consists of a Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, and 3 other members, all appointed by the President!
Their tenure and conditions of service are determined by the President through rules!
Functions
Monitors implementation of constitutional and legal safeguards for Scheduled Castes!
Inquires into specific complaints regarding deprivation of SC rights and safeguards!
Participates in and advises on the planning process for socio-economic development of SCs, and evaluates progress of such development!
Presents reports to the President on the working of these safeguards!
Report
NCSC submits an annual report, and additional reports as it deems necessary, to the President!
The President causes these reports to be laid before each House of Parliament, along with a memorandum explaining the action taken on the recommendations, and reasons for non-acceptance of any recommendation!
Powers
While investigating any specific complaint, NCSC has all the powers of a civil court — summoning and enforcing attendance of witnesses, requiring discovery/production of documents, and receiving evidence on affidavit!
Summary
NCSC evolved from a single "Special Officer" (original Art 338) → multi-member joint SC/ST Commission (65th Amendment 1990) → separate NCSC under Art 338 after bifurcation by the 89th Amendment 2003 (which created NCST separately under Art 338A). Composition = Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 members, President-appointed. Functions = monitoring safeguards, inquiring into complaints, advising on socio-economic development planning. Reports go to the President, then Parliament with an action-taken memorandum. Powers = civil court powers while investigating complaints.
