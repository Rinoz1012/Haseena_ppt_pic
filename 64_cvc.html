Main Notes
Establishment
CVC was originally set up in 1964 by an executive resolution of the government, based on the recommendations of the Santhanam Committee on Prevention of Corruption!
It was given statutory status only in 2003, through the Central Vigilance Commission Act, 2003!
Composition
Consists of a Central Vigilance Commissioner (Chairperson) and not more than 2 Vigilance Commissioners!
Appointed by the President on the recommendation of a committee comprising the Prime Minister, Union Home Minister, and Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha!
Function
Exercises superintendence over the functioning of the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI), but only in relation to investigation of offences under the Prevention of Corruption Act!
Oversees vigilance administration across Central Government ministries/departments/PSUs/nationalized banks!
Inquires or causes an inquiry/investigation into offences alleged under the Prevention of Corruption Act committed by certain categories of public servants!
Reviews the progress of investigations conducted by CBI into such offences, and tenders advice to the Central Government, PSUs, and other agencies on vigilance matters!
Jurisdiction
Covers Group A officers of the Central Government, and employees of Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) and nationalized banks above a certain designated level!
Working
CVC operates through a network of Chief Vigilance Officers (CVOs) appointed in each central government department/organization, who act as its extended arm for day-to-day vigilance functions!
Whistle Blowers Protection Act, 2014
Establishes a mechanism to receive complaints relating to corruption or wilful misuse of power/discretion against public servants!
Provides safeguards against victimization of the person making such a complaint (the whistle-blower)!
Designates the CVC as the "Competent Authority" to receive such complaints at the central government level!
Contains provisions for protecting the identity of the complainant, and prescribes penalties for those who make false or frivolous complaints!
Summary
CVC was set up in 1964 by executive resolution (Santhanam Committee recommendation) and given statutory status by the CVC Act, 2003. Composed of a Central Vigilance Commissioner + up to 2 Vigilance Commissioners, appointed by the President on a PM-Home Minister-LOP committee's recommendation. Its functions include superintending CBI (only in Prevention of Corruption Act matters), overseeing vigilance administration, reviewing investigations, and advising government/PSUs on vigilance matters — jurisdiction covers Group A officers and senior PSU/bank employees, operationalized through CVOs in each organization. The Whistle Blowers Protection Act, 2014 designates CVC as the competent authority to receive corruption-related complaints, with identity-protection safeguards and penalties for false complaints.
