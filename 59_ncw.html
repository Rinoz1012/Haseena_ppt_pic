Main Notes
Establishment & Composition
National Commission for Women (NCW) was established under the National Commission for Women Act, 1990 — a statutory body!
Composition: a Chairperson nominated by the Central Government, committed to the cause of women; 5 members nominated from persons of ability, integrity, and standing with expertise in law, trade unionism, industry, women's voluntary organisations, public administration, and economic development — with at least one member each from SC and ST communities; and a Member-Secretary, either an expert in management/organizational structure/sociological movement, or an officer of the IAS or equivalent rank!
Functions
Investigates and examines matters relating to safeguards provided for women under the Constitution and other laws!
Presents reports to the Central Government on the implementation of these safeguards!
Reviews existing legislation affecting women and recommends amendments to address gaps, inadequacies, or shortcomings!
Takes up cases of violation of women's rights with appropriate authorities, and looks into complaints received!
Undertakes promotional and educational research, and participates in/advises on planning of socio-economic development of women, evaluating progress made!
Inspects jails, remand homes, and other institutions/places where women are kept as prisoners or otherwise, and takes up matters for remedial action!
Powers
While investigating any complaint, NCW has all the powers of a civil court — summoning and enforcing attendance of witnesses, examining them under oath, requiring discovery/production of documents, and requisitioning public records!
Working
A dedicated Complaints and Investigation Cell processes complaints received by the Commission, either suo motu or from petitions!
Parivarik Mahila Lok Adalat
NCW facilitates the organization of Parivarik Mahila Lok Adalats — a form of Lok Adalat/alternative dispute resolution mechanism specifically for family and matrimonial disputes!
These aim to provide speedy, low-cost, and informal resolution of women's family/matrimonial grievances through counseling and conciliation, rather than lengthy formal litigation!
Summary
NCW (National Commission for Women Act, 1990) is a statutory body with a Chairperson + 5 members (incl. mandatory SC/ST representation) + Member-Secretary. Functions include investigating safeguards for women, reviewing/recommending legislative amendments, taking up complaints, evaluating socio-economic development, and inspecting institutions housing women. It has civil-court investigative powers, processes complaints through a dedicated cell, and facilitates Parivarik Mahila Lok Adalats — an alternative dispute resolution mechanism for speedy, conciliation-based resolution of family/matrimonial disputes.
