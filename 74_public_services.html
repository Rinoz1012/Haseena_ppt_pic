Main Notes
Classification of Services
All-India Services — such as the IAS, IPS, and Indian Forest Service — are common to both the Union and the States; officers are recruited and their basic service structure is controlled by the Union, but they serve in state cadres and hold key positions in both central and state administration!
Central Services — Group A, B, and C services meant for Union government departments and offices, e.g. Indian Foreign Service, Indian Revenue Service!
State Services — Group A, B, C, and D services for state government departments, recruited primarily through the State Public Service Commission!
Constitutional Provisions
Part XIV of the Constitution (Art 308-323) deals with "Services under the Union and the States"!
Art 309 empowers Parliament and State Legislatures to regulate recruitment and conditions of service of persons appointed to public services by law; until such a law is enacted, the President or the Governor may make rules for the purpose!
Art 310 embodies the doctrine of pleasure — civil servants, with certain specific exceptions, hold office during the pleasure of the President (for Union services) or the Governor (for state services)!
Art 311 provides key safeguards against arbitrary dismissal, removal, or reduction in rank — such action can only be taken by an authority not subordinate to the one that made the appointment, and the civil servant must be given a reasonable opportunity to show cause against the proposed action!
This opportunity requirement does NOT apply in three specific situations: where the person is dismissed on the ground of conduct which led to conviction on a criminal charge; where the disciplinary authority is satisfied that giving such an opportunity is not expedient for reasons connected with the security of the state; or where it is not reasonably practicable to hold such an inquiry!
Art 312 empowers Parliament to create a new All-India Service common to the Union and the States, but only if the Rajya Sabha passes a resolution, by a two-thirds majority of members present and voting, declaring that it is necessary or expedient in the national interest to do so!
Summary
Public services in India are classified into All-India Services (common to Union and states, e.g. IAS/IPS), Central Services (Union departments), and State Services (state departments). Governed by Part XIV (Art 308-323): Art 309 lets legislatures/executives regulate recruitment and service conditions; Art 310 embodies the doctrine of pleasure (tenure at the President's/Governor's pleasure); Art 311 safeguards against arbitrary dismissal, requiring a reasonable opportunity to be heard (with three specific exceptions — criminal conviction, state security, or impracticability of inquiry); and Art 312 allows Parliament to create new All-India Services, but only after the Rajya Sabha passes a resolution with a two-thirds majority declaring it necessary in the national interest.
