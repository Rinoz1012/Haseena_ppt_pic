Main Notes
Appointment & Term
Art 76 provides for the Attorney General of India (AG), the highest law officer in the country!
Appointed by the President; must possess the qualifications required to be appointed a Supreme Court judge!
Constitution does not fix a term — the AG holds office during the pleasure of the President, and can be removed anytime!
Remuneration determined by the President — not specified in the Constitution itself!
Duties & Functions
Gives advice to the Government of India on legal matters referred to him by the President!
Performs other duties of a legal character assigned by the President!
Appears on behalf of the Government of India in all cases in the Supreme Court and High Courts in which the Government is concerned!
Represents the Government of India in references made to the Supreme Court under Art 143 (President's power to seek SC's advisory opinion)!
Rights & Limitations
Has the right to speak and take part in the proceedings of both Houses of Parliament, joint sittings, and any Parliamentary Committee of which he may be named a member — but has NO right to vote!
Entitled to all privileges and immunities available to a Member of Parliament!
Limitations — cannot advise or hold a brief against the Government of India; cannot defend an accused person in criminal proceedings without the government's prior permission; cannot accept a directorship in any company without government permission!
Solicitor General of India
The Solicitor General and Additional Solicitors General assist the Attorney General in discharging his official duties!
These posts are NOT mentioned in the Constitution — they are statutory/administrative appointments, unlike the Attorney General who is a constitutional functionary under Art 76!
Summary
Attorney General of India (Art 76) is the highest law officer, appointed by the President (must be SC-judge qualified), holding office at the President's pleasure with no fixed term. Duties = advising government on referred legal matters, appearing for the Government in SC/HCs, and representing India in Art 143 references. Rights = can speak in both Houses/joint sittings/committees with MP-like privileges, but no voting right. Limitations = cannot act against the Government, defend criminal accused, or take directorships without permission. The Solicitor General and Additional Solicitors General assist the AG but are statutory posts, not constitutional ones.
