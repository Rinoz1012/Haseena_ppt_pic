Main Notes
Architecture
This period saw a distinctive fusion of Turkish/Islamic architectural elements (such as arches and domes) with existing Indian architectural traditions and craftsmanship, producing a new syncretic architectural style associated with the Delhi Sultanate period!
Religious Ideas and Beliefs
This era was characterized by significant coexistence and interaction between Hindu and Islamic religious traditions, setting important precedents for religious and cultural exchange in India!
The Sufi Movement
Sufism, the mystical dimension of Islam, emphasized personal spiritual devotion, divine love, and generally greater religious tolerance, and gained a substantial following in India during this period!
The Chishti and Suharwardi Silsilahs
These were two major and influential Sufi orders (silsilahs) active in India, each attracting devoted followings and playing significant roles in the era's religious and cultural life!
The Bhakti Movement and Vaishnavite Movement
This Hindu devotional tradition emphasized intense personal devotion to a chosen deity, and notably often transcended rigid caste distinctions, appealing across social strata!
The Vaishnavite Movement was a particularly significant strand within the broader Bhakti tradition, centered on devotion to Vishnu and his various avatars!
Literature and Fine Arts
This period saw considerable flourishing of regional language literature, poetry, and various art forms, reflecting the era's rich and diverse cultural developments!
Summary
Cultural developments between 1200-1500 were marked by significant religious and artistic synthesis — architecturally through the fusion of Islamic and Indian styles, and religiously through the coexistence of Hindu and Islamic traditions. The Sufi movement (notably the Chishti and Suharwardi orders) promoted mystical devotion and tolerance within Islam, while the Bhakti movement (including its significant Vaishnavite strand) promoted intense personal Hindu devotion that often transcended caste barriers. This period also saw considerable flourishing of regional literature and fine arts.
