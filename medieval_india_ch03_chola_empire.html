Main Notes
The Rise of the Chola Empire
The Cholas rose from their base in the Tamil region, gradually eclipsing the earlier dominant powers (Pallavas and Pandyas) to establish one of the most powerful and long-lasting empires in South Indian history!
Age of Rajaraja and Rajendra I
Rajaraja I significantly expanded Chola power and constructed the magnificent Brihadeeswara Temple at Thanjavur!
His son Rajendra I extended this further, notably undertaking a remarkable naval expedition against the Srivijaya kingdom in Southeast Asia, demonstrating the Cholas' unusual maritime and naval strength compared to most contemporary Indian dynasties!
Chola Government — Local Self-Government
The Cholas are especially renowned for their sophisticated system of local self-government. Village-level administrative bodies — the Ur (general village assembly), Sabha (assembly in Brahmadeya/Brahmin villages), and Nadu (a grouping of villages) — managed local affairs including irrigation, taxation, and dispute resolution!
The Uttaramerur inscriptions provide detailed evidence of village assembly elections and functioning, considered remarkably advanced for the period!
Cultural Life
The Chola period represents a peak of South Indian temple architecture (Dravidian style), exemplified by the Brihadeeswara Temple!
This era is also celebrated for exquisite Chola bronze sculptures, most famously the Nataraja (dancing Shiva) images, and for the flourishing and spread of Tamil literary and cultural traditions!
Summary
The Chola Empire (900-1200 CE) reached its zenith under Rajaraja I and Rajendra I, the latter notable for extending Chola naval power as far as Southeast Asia (Srivijaya). The Cholas are particularly celebrated for their sophisticated system of local self-government through village assemblies (Ur, Sabha, Nadu), documented in detail through inscriptions like those at Uttaramerur. Their cultural legacy includes peak Dravidian temple architecture (Brihadeeswara Temple) and renowned bronze sculpture (Nataraja images), marking this as a golden period for South Indian civilization.
