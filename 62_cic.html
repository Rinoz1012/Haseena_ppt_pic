Main Notes
Composition
CIC was established under the Right to Information Act, 2005, as the apex appellate authority for RTI matters concerning Union government bodies!
Consists of a Chief Information Commissioner and up to 10 Information Commissioners!
Appointed by the President on the recommendation of a committee comprising the Prime Minister (Chairperson), the Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha, and a Union Cabinet Minister nominated by the PM!
Tenure & Service Conditions
Under the original RTI Act 2005, CIC and Information Commissioners had a fixed 5-year term (or until age 65, whichever earlier), and their salary was pegged to that of an Election Commissioner!
The RTI (Amendment) Act, 2019 removed this fixed term — the term of office is now such period as may be PRESCRIBED BY THE CENTRAL GOVERNMENT through rules!
Similarly, salary, allowances, and other service conditions are now determined by the Central Government, rather than being tied to Election Commissioner-level parity!
This change has been criticized as potentially undermining the independence of CIC by giving the executive greater control over its functionaries' tenure and conditions!
Powers & Functions
Receives and inquires into complaints from persons who have been unable to file RTI requests, denied information, received incomplete/false/misleading information, or faced unreasonable fees!
Can order an inquiry into any matter if there are reasonable grounds, and while inquiring, has powers similar to a civil court — summoning persons, requiring production of documents, receiving evidence on affidavit, requisitioning public records!
Can impose penalties on Public Information Officers (PIOs) for unjustified refusal to receive an application, unreasonable delay, malafide denial, or providing incorrect/incomplete/misleading information!
Can recommend disciplinary action against an erring PIO under the applicable service rules!
Monitors and reports on the implementation of the RTI Act by publishing an annual report, which is laid before Parliament!
Summary
CIC (RTI Act, 2005) is composed of a Chief Information Commissioner + up to 10 Information Commissioners, appointed by the President on the recommendation of a committee (PM, LOP-LS, Cabinet Minister). Originally, tenure was a fixed 5 years with salary pegged to an Election Commissioner, but the RTI (Amendment) Act, 2019 removed the fixed term and shifted both tenure and service conditions to be determined by the Central Government via rules — a change criticized for reducing CIC's independence. Powers/functions include receiving and inquiring into complaints, exercising civil-court-like investigative powers, imposing penalties on non-compliant PIOs, recommending disciplinary action, and publishing an annual implementation report.
