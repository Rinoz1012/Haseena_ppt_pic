Main Notes
Separate Commission for STs
Before 2003, Scheduled Tribes' safeguards were monitored by the same joint National Commission for SCs and STs (created under Art 338 by the 65th Amendment, 1990)!
The 89th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2003 inserted a new Art 338A, establishing a SEPARATE National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST), distinct from NCSC!
Rationale — the distinct developmental and administrative needs of tribal communities (e.g. Fifth/Sixth Schedule areas) warranted a dedicated body!
Composition
NCST consists of a Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, and 3 other members, appointed by the President, with tenure/conditions determined by the President!
Function
Monitors implementation of constitutional and legal safeguards for Scheduled Tribes!
Inquires into specific complaints relating to deprivation of ST rights and safeguards!
Participates in and advises on planning for the socio-economic development of STs, evaluating the progress made!
Other Function
NCST additionally has an advisory role connected to the administration of Scheduled Areas (5th Schedule) and Tribal Areas (6th Schedule)!
May be consulted on matters relating to tribal welfare policy, including issues arising from implementation of laws like PESA (1996)!
Report
Submits reports to the President, who lays them before Parliament along with a memorandum on the action taken and reasons for non-acceptance of recommendations, if any!
Powers
While investigating complaints, NCST enjoys the same civil court powers as NCSC — summoning witnesses, requiring production of documents, receiving evidence on affidavit!
Summary
NCST (Art 338A, inserted by the 89th Amendment 2003) emerged from bifurcating the earlier joint SC/ST Commission, giving Scheduled Tribes their own dedicated body. Composition = Chairperson + Vice-Chairperson + 3 members, President-appointed. Functions = monitoring ST safeguards, inquiring into complaints, advising on socio-economic development planning. Additional function = advisory link to 5th/6th Schedule administration and tribal welfare policy (incl. PESA-related matters). Reports go to the President, then Parliament with an action-taken memorandum; powers = civil court powers while investigating.
