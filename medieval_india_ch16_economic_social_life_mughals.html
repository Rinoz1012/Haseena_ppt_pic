Main Notes
Economic and Social Conditions; Standard of Living
Mughal India's economy and society exhibited a considerable gap between the ruling elite's prosperity and the generally modest standard of living experienced by the common rural masses, whose lives were shaped by traditional village patterns of agriculture and social organization!
The Ruling Classes — Nobles and Zamindars
The Mughal nobility formed a privileged and wealthy elite class, holding significant administrative and military authority, often granted extensive lands and resources by the emperor in exchange for service!
Zamindars functioned as an important intermediary landholding class between the Mughal state and the peasantry, collecting revenue and exercising considerable local authority and influence!
The Middle Strata
An emerging middle strata occupied intermediate social and economic positions within Mughal society, distinct from both the ruling elite and the general peasantry!
Organization of Trade and Commerce
Mughal India possessed well-developed and organized trade networks, both domestic and connecting to broader international commercial systems!
Foreign Trade and the European Traders
This period saw the growing involvement of European trading companies (Portuguese, Dutch, English, French) in Indian commerce, gradually establishing an increasingly significant presence in India's foreign trade!
Summary
Economic and social life under the Mughals featured a stark contrast between the wealthy ruling classes (nobles and zamindars, who held significant land and authority) and the generally modest standard of living among the rural masses following traditional village patterns. An emerging middle strata occupied intermediate positions in this social structure. Trade and commerce were well-organized both domestically and internationally, with this period notably marking the growing involvement of European trading companies in Indian foreign trade — a development that would have profound long-term consequences for India's political and economic future.
