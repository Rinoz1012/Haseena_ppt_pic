Main Notes
Appointment & Term
Art 165 provides for the Advocate General of a State, the state-level counterpart of the Attorney General of India!
Appointed by the Governor of the state; must possess qualifications required to be appointed a High Court judge!
No fixed term specified in the Constitution — holds office during the pleasure of the Governor, and may be removed anytime!
Remuneration determined by the Governor!
Duties & Rights
Duty to give advice to the State Government on legal matters referred by the Governor, and to perform other duties of a legal character assigned to him!
Has the right to speak and take part in proceedings of the State Legislature (both Houses, where bicameral) and any committee of which he is named a member — but has NO right to vote!
Enjoys privileges and immunities available to a member of the State Legislature while performing these functions!
Summary
Advocate General of a State (Art 165) mirrors the Attorney General at the state level — appointed by the Governor (must be HC-judge qualified), holding office at the Governor's pleasure with no fixed term. Duties = advising the State Government on referred legal matters and performing assigned legal duties. Rights = can speak in the State Legislature and its committees with member-like privileges, but no voting right.
