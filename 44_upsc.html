Main Notes
Composition
UPSC is a Central Recruiting Agency established under Art 315, consisting of a Chairman and other members, the number of which is determined by the President!
Constitution mandates that at least half the members should be persons who have held office for at least 10 years under the Government of India or a State!
Appointed by the President; term = 6 years or till 65 years of age, whichever is earlier!
Removal
Art 317: Chairman/members removed only by the President!
On ground of misbehaviour — President must refer the matter to the Supreme Court for inquiry; removal only if SC finds misbehaviour proved!
On other grounds (insolvency, engaging in paid employment outside official duties, infirmity of mind/body) — President can remove directly, no SC inquiry needed!
Independence
Security of tenure; service conditions cannot be varied to their disadvantage after appointment!
Expenditure of UPSC (salaries, allowances) charged on the Consolidated Fund of India — not subject to annual vote by Parliament!
Post-retirement restriction: Chairman not eligible for further employment under Govt of India/State except as Chairman/Member of UPSC or SPSC; a Member is eligible to be appointed Chairman of UPSC/SPSC but not for other government employment!
Functions
Conducts examinations for appointments to All-India Services and Central Services (Group A & B)!
Advises the government on recruitment methods, principles for promotions and transfers, and disciplinary matters!
Advises on claims for reimbursement of legal costs incurred by civil servants in defending official actions, and on pension-related matters!
Assists states, on request, in framing and operating joint recruitment schemes!
Limitations
UPSC's role is purely advisory — its recommendations are NOT binding on the Government!
Does not cover recruitment to Group C & D posts, or temporary/officiating appointments!
Not consulted on matters relating to reservation policy for SC/ST/OBC in appointments!
Role
Acts as the "watchdog of merit system" in India, safeguarding recruitment and service conditions from political/administrative interference!
Joint State Public Service Commission
Art 315(2)-(4): Parliament may establish a Joint State Public Service Commission (JSPSC) for two or more states, but only upon request through resolutions passed by the concerned state legislatures!
Members of JSPSC appointed by the President; term same as UPSC/SPSC (6 years/65 yrs)!
Summary
UPSC (Art 315-323) = central recruiting agency; Chairman+members appointed by President, ≥1/2 must have 10 yrs govt experience; term 6yrs/65yrs. Removal (Art 317) only by President — misbehaviour needs SC inquiry, other grounds (insolvency, outside employment, infirmity) don't. Independence via tenure security, non-variable service conditions, expenditure charged on Consolidated Fund, and post-retirement employment bar. Functions (Art 320) = conducting civil service exams, advising on recruitment/promotion/discipline — but purely advisory, not binding, and excludes Group C/D posts & reservation matters. JSPSC (Art 315(2)-(4)) = Parliament-created joint commission for 2+ states on their request.
