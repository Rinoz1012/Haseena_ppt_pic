Main Notes
Establishment & Rationale
National Investigation Agency (NIA) was established under the National Investigation Agency Act, 2008, enacted in the aftermath of the 2008 Mumbai (26/11) terror attacks!
Rationale — terrorism and other national-security-related offences often span multiple states and require specialized, uniform investigation beyond the capacity/jurisdiction of individual state police forces!
Functions
Investigates and prosecutes "Scheduled Offences" listed in the Schedule to the NIA Act — covering terrorism, insurgency, and related offences under various special laws (e.g., Unlawful Activities Prevention Act, Atomic Energy Act)!
Can take up such cases suo motu, and also assists state governments and other agencies upon their request!
Vision & Mission
Vision — to become a highly trusted national agency, respected for combating terrorism and other threats to national security!
Mission — to set the highest standards of excellence in counter-terrorism and other national-security-related investigation at the national level, through effective and competent investigation!
Jurisdiction
NIA's jurisdiction extends throughout India, and, unlike the CBI, does NOT require the concerned state government's consent to investigate Scheduled Offences!
Following the 2019 Amendment, jurisdiction was extended to Indian citizens and officials abroad, and to offences committed against Indian citizens or Indian interests outside India!
NIA (Amendment) Act, 2019
Expanded the list of Scheduled Offences to include human trafficking, offences related to counterfeit currency, manufacture/sale of prohibited arms, cyber-terrorism, and certain offences under the Explosive Substances Act!
Extended NIA's jurisdiction to investigate offences committed outside India, subject to relevant international treaties and the domestic law of the concerned country!
Allowed the Central Government to set up Special Courts for the trial of NIA cases, with the concerned state's concurrence required when a High Court judge is to be designated as the special court judge!
Summary
NIA (NIA Act, 2008) was established post-26/11 as a specialized central agency for terrorism and national-security offences, given the inter-state limitations of state police. Functions include investigating/prosecuting Scheduled Offences (suo motu or on request), guided by a vision/mission centered on excellence in counter-terrorism investigation. Unlike CBI, NIA does not need state consent to investigate, and jurisdiction covers all of India plus (post-2019) offences against Indian citizens/interests abroad. The NIA (Amendment) Act, 2019 expanded Scheduled Offences (human trafficking, counterfeit currency, cyber-terrorism, etc.), extended extraterritorial jurisdiction, and enabled Special Courts for NIA cases.
