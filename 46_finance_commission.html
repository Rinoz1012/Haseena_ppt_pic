Main Notes
Composition
Art 280 provides for a Finance Commission to be constituted by the President every 5 years, or earlier if considered necessary!
Consists of a Chairman and four other members, appointed by the President!
Qualifications of members prescribed by Parliament through the Finance Commission Act, 1951!
Functions
Recommends the distribution of net proceeds of taxes to be shared between the Centre and States (vertical devolution), and their allocation among the States (horizontal devolution)!
Recommends principles governing grants-in-aid to states out of the Consolidated Fund of India (Art 275)!
Recommends measures needed to augment the Consolidated Fund of a State to supplement resources of Panchayats & Municipalities, based on the recommendations of the State Finance Commission!
Addresses any other matter referred to it by the President in the interest of sound finance!
Advisory Role
The Finance Commission's recommendations are advisory in nature and not binding on the government!
By strong convention, the Union government has generally accepted its recommendations, giving them practical, quasi-binding weight!
The report is submitted to the President, who causes it to be laid before Parliament along with an explanatory memorandum on the action taken!
Summary
Finance Commission (Art 280) = quasi-judicial body constituted by the President every 5 years (Chairman + 4 members, qualifications per Finance Commission Act 1951). Functions = recommending vertical & horizontal tax devolution between Centre-States and among states, grants-in-aid under Art 275, measures to augment states' funds for Panchayats/Municipalities based on SFC inputs, and any other President-referred matter. Its role is purely advisory — recommendations aren't legally binding but are conventionally accepted; report goes to the President, tabled in Parliament with an action-taken memorandum.
