Main Notes
Rationale of Special Provisions
Part XVI (Art 330-342) provides special provisions for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Backward Classes, and (historically) Anglo-Indians, aimed at ensuring adequate representation in the legislature and public services, and promoting social justice through protective discrimination for historically disadvantaged sections!
Specification of Classes
Art 341 empowers the President to specify, through a public notification, the castes/races/tribes to be deemed Scheduled Castes for a particular state or Union Territory, after consulting the Governor (for a state)!
Once such a list is notified, it can only be modified — inclusion or exclusion of any caste — through a LAW enacted by Parliament, not by any subsequent order of the President!
Art 342 makes similar provisions for the specification of Scheduled Tribes!
Special Provision for SCs and STs
Art 330 provides for reservation of seats for SCs and STs in the Lok Sabha in proportion to their population; Art 332 makes a similar provision for State Legislative Assemblies!
This reservation was originally meant to last for 10 years but has been repeatedly extended through successive constitutional amendments — most recently by the 104th Amendment Act, 2019, which extended it until 2030!
Art 335 provides that the claims of SC/ST members shall be taken into consideration in making appointments to services and posts, consistent with the maintenance of efficiency of administration; a proviso added by the 82nd Amendment Act, 2000 allows for relaxation in qualifying marks or standards of evaluation in matters relating to reservation in promotions!
Special Provision for BCs
Art 340 empowers the President to appoint a Commission to investigate the conditions of socially and educationally backward classes and recommend steps for their advancement!
This provision formed the constitutional basis for the Mandal Commission, appointed in 1979, whose recommendations for 27% reservation for Other Backward Classes (OBCs) in central government jobs were implemented in 1990!
Special Provision for Anglo-Indians
Originally, Art 331 (for the Lok Sabha) and Art 333 (for State Legislative Assemblies) allowed the President to nominate up to 2 members, and the Governor to nominate 1 member respectively, from the Anglo-Indian community, if it was found that the community was not adequately represented!
The 104th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2019 ABOLISHED this nomination provision for Anglo-Indians, with effect from 25 January 2020, ending a practice that had existed since the Constitution's adoption — this change was bundled together with the extension of SC/ST reservation in the same amendment!
Summary
Part XVI (Art 330-342) provides special provisions to ensure representation and social justice for SC, ST, BC, and (historically) Anglo-Indian communities. Art 341/342 empower the President to specify SC/ST lists (modifiable only by Parliament's law thereafter). SC/ST get reserved seats in the Lok Sabha (Art 330) and State Assemblies (Art 332), extended till 2030 by the 104th Amendment 2019; Art 335 balances their claims in appointments against administrative efficiency, with a relaxation proviso (82nd Amendment 2000) for promotions. Art 340 enables a Commission to investigate backward classes' conditions — the basis for the Mandal Commission (1979) and 27% OBC reservation (1990). Anglo-Indians originally had nomination rights to the Lok Sabha (Art 331) and State Assemblies (Art 333), which were abolished by the 104th Amendment Act, 2019, effective 25 Jan 2020.
