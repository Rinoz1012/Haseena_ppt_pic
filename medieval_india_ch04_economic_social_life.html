Main Notes
Trade and Commerce
This period saw active trade both within India and overseas. Arab traders were prominent along India's western coast, while eastern and southern regions maintained strong maritime links with Southeast Asia!
Internal trade was supported by organized merchant guilds in various regions!
Nature of Society and the Caste System
Society remained fundamentally hierarchical and caste-based, though with considerable regional variation in how caste operated!
During this period, the caste system grew increasingly rigid, with numerous sub-castes (jatis) proliferating, often linked closely to specific occupations!
Condition of Women
Women's social position was largely restrictive during this period. Practices such as sati (though not universal) and various forms of purdah/seclusion existed among certain regions and social classes, though the degree and nature of restriction varied considerably across different parts of India and different social strata!
Dress, Food, and Amusements
Considerable regional diversity existed in dress and food habits. Common amusements, particularly among ruling elites, included wrestling, hunting, music, and dance performances!
Education, Science and Religious Learning
Education was largely centered around temples and monasteries, with learning conducted in Sanskrit as well as regional languages. Some rulers extended patronage to notable centers of learning during this period!
Religious Movements and Beliefs
This period marks the early stirrings of the Bhakti devotional tradition, which would grow enormously in subsequent centuries!
Both Shaivism and Vaishnavism flourished as major devotional traditions, while Jainism and Buddhism, though in decline relative to their earlier prominence, persisted in certain regional pockets!
Summary
Between 800-1200 CE, Indian society remained hierarchical and increasingly caste-rigid, with women facing largely restrictive social conditions (varying regionally). Trade flourished both overland and via sea (particularly with Arab traders in the west and Southeast Asia in the east/south), supported by merchant guilds. Education centered on temples and monasteries, while religious life saw the early growth of Bhakti devotionalism alongside established Shaiva and Vaishnava traditions, even as Buddhism and Jainism receded from their earlier prominence.
