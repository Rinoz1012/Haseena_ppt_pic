Main Notes
Establishment
Bar Council of India (BCI) was established under the Advocates Act, 1961 — the statutory, apex body regulating the legal profession and legal education in India!
Composition
Consists of the Attorney General of India and the Solicitor General of India as ex-officio members, along with one member elected by each State Bar Council from among its own members!
The Chairman and Vice-Chairman are elected by the Council from among its members!
Functions
Lays down standards of professional conduct and etiquette for advocates, and prescribes the procedure to be followed by its disciplinary committees and those of State Bar Councils!
Safeguards the rights, privileges, and interests of advocates, and promotes and supports law reform!
Deals with and disposes of matters of professional misconduct through disciplinary committees, or on appeal from State Bar Councils!
Promotes legal education and lays down standards for it in consultation with universities and State Bar Councils; recognizes universities whose law degree qualifies a person for enrolment as an advocate!
Organizes seminars and talks on legal topics, publishes journals and papers of legal interest, and organizes legal aid for the poor!
Recognizes foreign law qualifications for limited purposes, and manages and invests the funds of the Council!
State Bar Councils
Statutory bodies established under the Advocates Act for each state (or a group of states/UTs)!
Functions parallel to BCI's, at the state level — admitting advocates to the roll, preparing/maintaining the roll of advocates, entertaining and determining cases of misconduct, safeguarding advocates' rights, organizing legal aid, managing/investing funds, and electing members to represent the state on the BCI!
Types of Advocates
Senior Advocates — designated by the Supreme Court or a High Court, in recognition of their ability, experience, and standing at the Bar; they are subject to certain restrictions (e.g., generally cannot file affidavits or draft pleadings directly for a client without an accompanying junior advocate)!
Other Advocates — regular advocates enrolled with a State Bar Council, who do not carry the "Senior" designation!
Summary
BCI (Advocates Act, 1961) is the statutory apex body regulating the legal profession and legal education, composed of the AG and SG of India (ex-officio) plus one elected member from each State Bar Council, with a Chairman/Vice-Chairman elected from among members. Functions include setting professional conduct standards, handling disciplinary matters, promoting legal education, recognizing law degree-granting universities, and organizing legal aid. State Bar Councils perform parallel functions at the state level and elect BCI's members. Advocates are categorized as Senior Advocates (court-designated, with certain restrictions) and Other Advocates (regular enrolled practitioners).
